FDA-Approved GLP-1 Medications

Breakthrough weight loss medications — personalized to you.

💉 Weekly Injection · GLP-1
Wegovy®
Semaglutide 2.4 mg
15%
avg. body weight lost in trials
Semaglutide mimics the natural GLP-1 hormone, binding to receptors in the pancreas, stomach, and brain. This leads to increased insulin sensitivity, slowed gastric emptying, appetite suppression, and enhanced satiety.
Along with diet and exercise, patients achieved an average of 15% weight loss maintained over 2 years. The SELECT trial also demonstrated a 20% reduction in major cardiovascular events.
Nausea, vomiting, constipation, bloating, gas, gastritis, and fatigue — most common during initiation and dose escalation, typically improving over time.
Individuals with personal or family history of Medullary Thyroid Cancer (MTC) or MEN2 Syndrome. Those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or trying to conceive.
💉 Weekly Injection · GLP-1 + GIP
Zepbound®
Tirzepatide 2.5–15 mg
21%
avg. body weight lost in trials
Tirzepatide activates both GLP-1 and GIP receptors — a dual mechanism that slows gastric emptying, reduces cravings, and helps the body break down stored fat more efficiently than single-receptor medications.
Patients experienced up to 22% weight loss with tirzepatide. A study of 18,000 participants showed tirzepatide (42%) was more effective than semaglutide (18%) in achieving ≥15% weight loss. Also FDA-approved for obstructive sleep apnea.
Nausea, vomiting, constipation, diarrhea, bloating, gas, gastritis, and fatigue — typically most pronounced during initiation and dose escalation.
Individuals with personal or family history of Medullary Thyroid Cancer (MTC) or MEN2 Syndrome. Those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or trying to conceive.
💊 Daily Pill · No Injection · GLP-1
Oral Wegovy®
Semaglutide 25 mg Tablet
15%
avg. body weight lost in trials
Same active ingredient as injectable Wegovy — semaglutide — but in a once-daily oral tablet. Works by mimicking the GLP-1 hormone to reduce appetite, increase satiety, and support steady weight loss without any needles.
Take once daily on an empty stomach with no more than 4 oz of plain water. Wait 30 minutes before eating, drinking, or taking other medications. Consistent timing is critical for optimal absorption.
Nausea, upset stomach, and diarrhea — most common at initiation. Generally milder than the injectable form for many patients.
Personal or family history of MTC or MEN2 Syndrome. Pregnancy, breastfeeding, or actively trying to conceive. Those who cannot take medication on an empty stomach consistently.
💊 Daily Pill · No Injection · GLP-1
Foundayo®
Orforglipron
11%
avg. body weight lost in trials
Foundayo is a non-peptide, oral GLP-1 receptor agonist — meaning it works on the same hunger and metabolism pathways as injectable GLP-1s, but as a simple daily pill with no food or water restrictions required.
Unlike Oral Wegovy, Foundayo can be taken with or without food and with any beverage — making it the most flexible oral GLP-1 option currently available. Ideal for patients whose lifestyle makes the strict Oral Wegovy requirements difficult.
Nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, and constipation — similar to other GLP-1 medications, typically mild and improving over time.
Personal or family history of MTC or MEN2 Syndrome. Pregnancy or breastfeeding. Your provider will review your full medical history to confirm eligibility.

Metformin
Biguanide — insulin sensitizer
2–5%
avg. weight loss, gradual & long-term
Metformin helps your body use insulin more efficiently, reducing fat storage and supporting stable energy levels. When combined with GLP-1 medications, it can enhance appetite control, blood sugar regulation, and fat metabolism — helping you reach your goals more effectively.
Modest weight loss of 2–5% of body weight, gradual over months and sustainable long-term — especially in those with PCOS, insulin resistance, prediabetes, or diabetes. Patients often notice steadier energy, fewer sugar cravings, and better blood sugar control within days to weeks.

💉 Injection · Energy & Longevity
NAD+
Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide
Energy
clarity, recovery & healthy aging
NAD+ is a natural molecule found in every cell that helps create energy, repair cells, and keep you healthy. As we age, NAD+ levels drop — leaving us tired, foggy, or slower to recover. NAD+ injections deliver this molecule directly into your system for better absorption, helping cells produce energy more efficiently and supporting healthy aging.
Most people notice more energy, clearer thinking, better mood, and faster recovery. Supporting NAD+ levels may also make it easier to burn fat and stay active while improving long-term wellness.
Mild redness or warmth at the injection site, brief headache, or lightheadedness — typically minimal and short-lived.
Those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have active cancer should avoid NAD+ therapy. Your provider will review your history before prescribing.
💉 Injection · Growth Hormone Support
Sermorelin
GHRH analog — 29 amino acids
3–6 mo
avg. time to see clinical improvement
Sermorelin gently stimulates your pituitary gland to release your own natural growth hormone — rather than replacing it externally. Growth hormone levels naturally decline with age. Restoring them supports improved metabolism, lean muscle preservation, fat reduction, better sleep, and recovery — making it a powerful complement to GLP-1 therapy.
With regular use and a healthy lifestyle, many patients notice more energy, better sleep, improved mood, faster recovery, and easier weight management over time — particularly preservation of lean muscle mass while losing fat on GLP-1 therapy.
Mild redness or irritation at the injection site, temporary headache, or feeling tired after the first few doses — typically resolving within days.
Those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have active cancer should avoid Sermorelin. Also not recommended for untreated hypothyroidism. Your provider will review your full history before prescribing.

B12 injections deliver the vitamin directly into your muscle for quick, effective absorption — bypassing the digestive system entirely. This helps your body convert food into energy more efficiently, supports metabolism, boosts mood, and enhances mental clarity. Particularly important for patients on GLP-1 therapy who may be eating less and at risk of deficiency.
Many patients report feeling more energetic, focused, and alert within days to weeks. Regular B12 injections support metabolism, mood balance, and overall wellness — especially important for those following a vegetarian, low-meat, or calorie-restricted diet.
Mild redness or tenderness at the injection site, slight headache, or brief lightheadedness — all typically minimal and short-lived.
Individuals with known allergies to cobalt or vitamin B12, or those with Leber's disease (hereditary optic atrophy). Always inform your provider of all known allergies before starting.

Frequently Asked Questions

GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1) medications mimic the action of a naturally occurring hormone produced in the gut in response to food. This hormone stimulates insulin production, regulates blood sugar levels, slows gastric emptying, and signals the brain to reduce appetite and increase feelings of fullness.
GLP-1 medications are contraindicated in individuals with a personal or family history of Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC) or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2). They are also not recommended for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or actively trying to conceive. Your provider will review your full medical history before prescribing.
The most common side effects include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation — particularly during initiation and dose escalation. Patients may also experience fatigue or dizziness, especially if not staying well hydrated. These effects generally improve as the body adjusts to the medication over several weeks.
Sarcopenia is the loss of muscle mass that can occur with any form of calorie restriction — not just GLP-1 therapy. Patients on GLP-1 therapy, especially postmenopausal women, are at increased risk if weight loss is not supported by regular exercise and adequate protein intake. This is exactly why physician monitoring is essential throughout your treatment.
